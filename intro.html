Introduction to Integration

Integration is one of the most importatnt topics in calculus. It is the reverse process of differentiation and is used to find areas, volumes, central points, and other real-life applications.

This Website is designed to help students understand the fundamental concepts of integration, various techniques, and their applications.

Concepts and Methods of Integration

Integration deals with finding the integral of a function and is mainly divided into two types:
Indefinite and Definite Integrals.

Indefinite Integrals represent a family of functions and include a constant of integration (C).
Definite Integrals, on the other hand, calculate the area under a curve between two specified limits.

Various techniques of integration include Substitution, Integration by Parts, Partial Fractions, and Trigonometric Integrals.
Each technique is suited for different types of functions and problems.

Applications of integration are vast and include calculating areas, volumes, work done by a force, and solving differential equations.
