Applications of Integration

Integration has a wide range of applications in various fields such as physics, engineering, economics, and biology. Here are some common applications of integration:

1. Area Under a Curve

One of the most common applications of integration is to find the area under a curve defined by a function f(x) between two points a and b.
This is done using definite integrals.

2. Volume of Solids of Revolution

Integration is used to calculate the volume of solids formed by revolving a region around an axis. Methods such as the disk method and shell method are commonly used for this purpose.

3. Work Done by a Force

In physics, integration is used to calculate the work done by a variable force over a distance. The work done is given by the integral of the force function with respect to distance.

4. Center of Mass

Integration helps in determining the center of mass of an object with varying density. By integrating the mass distribution, one can find the coordinates of the center of mass.

5. Probability and Statistics

In probability theory, integration is used to find probabilities and expected values for continuous random variables by integrating their probability density functions.
